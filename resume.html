Overview

Hi, I'm Kiley. I'm a senior front-end engineer and I've been building web applications and delivering software projects since 2011. I'm engaged most when I get to work with a close-knit team building products that people use to solve important problems. I focus on building software that clearly solves a user's job-to-be-done while being secure, maintainable, performant, and easy to use.

My technical specialty as a front-end developer includes a deep expertise in the usual suspects like HTML, CSS, and JavaScript. I've built numerous production-grade, thick-client web applications using React, TypeScript, and APIs with both GraphQL and REST. Outside of front end, I've led efforts in cloud application development, API development, database design, user experience design, product design, and product strategy. I've also held several roles as a technical team lead, leading teams of both junior and senior engineers.

I care deeply about helping early-career developers through pair programming, mentoring, tech talks, and community building.

Background

I've designed and built over 15 SaaS applications over the past decade, from recruiting software to healthcare products to subscription-based services. A few highlights:

  • At AWS, working in the ProServe Application Development division, I built 2 full-stack, consumer-facing SaaS products for a Fortune 500 tire company; worked on a project with a multi-lingual team called "Gas Stations of the Future" for the largest energy company in Argentina; and was the tech lead for a multi-million dollar engagement building ML-powered data forecasting tools for the Amazon packaging innovation organization.
  • Later at AWS, working on an AWS service team, I made tooling improvements that reduced our monorepo build time by 28%, resulting in saving 80 engineers about 40 seconds per build. 40 seconds seems small, but with 80 engineers running 3-4 builds per day, it equates to almost 3 hours of engineering time saved per day.
  • At DentalPost, a job board software company based in Atlanta with $4M in annual revenue, I led the 4-person engineering team as the CTO, shipped a rebuild of the product from Java Server Pages to a Java REST API with a React front end, shifted engineering from scrum 2-week sprints to 6-week cycles using the "Shape Up" method, and helped the team ship monthly releases of a half-dozen long-awaited, big features.
  • At SOLTECH, a ~50-person software consulting firm based, I built a React Native front end of an iPad application that was used by doctors with patients who speak a different language. The app connected a live translator via video chat to translate between patient and doctor in realtime.
  • At TORQworks, a software company that helped long-term unemlpoyed individuals find new careers and jobs, I acted as the only front-end developer, UX designer, and UI designer. I redesigned what was an outdated web application using the (then) modern visual design system from Google called Material Design. I built the front-end of the new version using AngularJS, working closely with and learning a great deal from the CTO who was a former Microsoft software engineer (forever grateful for your mentorship and faith in me, JM!).
  • In my first job out of college, I worked at ACT Bridge, an HR technology startup funded by ACT (the college testing company). I started as the team's one-and-only "tech person" but eventually became the product lead as the company transformed from a pure consulting to a technology SaaS business. I envisioned and designed the SaaS product, oversaw its development, and launched it at a major healthcare provider.
